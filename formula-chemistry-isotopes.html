2. Isotopes & Average Atomic Mass

Overview

Isotope notation: AZX or X‑A (e.g., Carbon‑14).

Average atomic mass formula:

Average Mass = Σ (isotope mass × fractional abundance)

Pattern: Weighted average → convert % to decimal. Heavier isotope with higher percent pulls the average toward it.

Practice Questions

  1. 1. What term describes atoms of the same element that differ only in neutron count?

    Isotopes

  2. 2. If chlorine-35 is 75% abundant and chlorine-37 is 25% abundant, what is the average atomic mass?

    35.5 amu

  3. 3. What formula is used to calculate average atomic mass?

    Σ(mass × fractional abundance)

  4. 4. An element’s average atomic mass moves toward which isotope?

    The most abundant isotope

  5. 5. What isotope notation format is written as carbon-14?

    X–A format (element‑mass number)
