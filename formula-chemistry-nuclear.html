10. Nuclear Relationships

Overview

Mass defect: Δm = Z mp + N mn − matom.

Binding energy: E = (Δm) c2.

Pattern: More binding energy = more stable (iron peak = max stability).

Practice Questions

  1. 1. What do you call the mass difference between separated nucleons and the actual nucleus?

    Mass defect

  2. 2. What equation relates mass defect to binding energy?

    E = (Δm)c²

  3. 3. A nucleus with high binding energy per nucleon is considered what?

    Very stable

  4. 4. Which element sits at the peak of nuclear stability?

    Iron (Fe)

  5. 5. What term describes the energy required to break a nucleus into individual nucleons?

    Binding energy

Early-Buzz Patterns

  • "This mass difference creates binding energy…" → Mass defect
  • "This equation uses Δm and c²…" → E = (Δm)c²
  • "This quantity peaks for iron…" → Binding energy per nucleon
  • "Energy required to separate a nucleus…" → Binding energy
  • "This value equals sum of free nucleon masses minus actual mass…" → Mass defect
