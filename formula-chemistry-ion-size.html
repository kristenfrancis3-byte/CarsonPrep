7. Ion Size Patterns

Overview

Cations: smaller than neutral atom (lose electrons → shell contracts).

Anions: larger than neutral atom (gain electrons → increased electron–electron repulsion).

Isoelectronic series rule: same electrons → more protons = smaller (e.g., O2−, F−, Na+, Mg2+ → smallest = Mg2+).

Practice Questions

  1. 1. Are cations larger or smaller than their neutral atoms?

    Smaller

  2. 2. Are anions larger or smaller than their neutral atoms?

    Larger

  3. 3. In an isoelectronic series, what determines size?

    Number of protons (more protons = smaller)

  4. 4. Which is larger: S²⁻ or Cl⁻?

    S²⁻

  5. 5. Which is smaller: Na⁺ or Mg²⁺?

    Mg²⁺

Early-Buzz Patterns

  • "These ions are always smaller than their atoms…" → Cations
  • "These ions expand due to added electron repulsion…" → Anions
  • "Ranking atoms of equal electrons…" → Isoelectronic series
  • "More protons with same electrons means this…" → Smaller radius
  • "This ion type forms by gaining electrons…" → Anion
