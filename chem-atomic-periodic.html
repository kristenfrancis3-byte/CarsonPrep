Atomic Structure & Periodic Trends

Master the fundamentals of atomic structure, electron configurations, and periodic trends. These concepts form the foundation for understanding bonding, reactivity, and virtually every other topic in chemistry.

What Carson Must Know

Core Concepts

Subatomic Particles

  • Protons: Positively charged, determine atomic number (Z), mass ≈ 1 amu
  • Neutrons: Neutral, determine isotope, mass ≈ 1 amu
  • Electrons: Negatively charged, occupy orbitals, mass ≈ 1/1836 amu (negligible)

Atomic Number & Mass Number

  • Atomic Number (Z): Number of protons; defines the element
  • Mass Number (A): Protons + Neutrons
  • Isotopes: Same element, different neutrons, different mass numbers

Electron Configuration

Three Rules Govern Filling:

  • Aufbau Principle: Fill lowest-energy orbitals first (1s, 2s, 2p, 3s, 3p, 4s, 3d...)
  • Hund's Rule: Electrons singly occupy orbitals before pairing (maximize unpaired electrons)
  • Pauli Exclusion Principle: No two electrons have identical quantum numbers (opposite spins in same orbital)

Periodic Trends (Across a Period; Down a Group)

Atomic Radius Decreases left to right | Increases down
Ionization Energy Increases left to right | Decreases down
Electron Affinity Increases left to right | Decreases down
Electronegativity Increases left to right | Decreases down

Ion Size Rules

  • Cations are smaller than their neutral atoms (electrons lost, less shielding)
  • Anions are larger than their neutral atoms (electrons gained, more repulsion)
  • Within isoelectronic series: higher charge → smaller ion
Key Formulas

Equations You Need

Electromagnetic Radiation

c = λν

Where: c = speed of light (3 × 10⁸ m/s), λ = wavelength (m), ν = frequency (Hz)

Photon Energy

E = hν = hc/λ

Where: h = Planck's constant (6.626 × 10⁻³⁴ J·s)

Effective Nuclear Charge

Zeff = Z – S

Where: Z = atomic number, S = shielding constant (approximate electrons between nucleus and outer electron)

Buzzwords

Pattern Recognition Clues

Question Type Identifiers (First Few Words)

  • "This particle…" → Subatomic particle question (proton, neutron, electron)
  • "This rule states…" → Electron configuration rule (Aufbau, Hund, Pauli)
  • "This trend…" → Periodic trend (atomic radius, IE, EN, EA)
  • "As atomic number increases…" → Trend analysis
  • "Weighted average…" → Atomic mass calculation

Instant Answer Triggers

  • Opposite spins → Pauli Exclusion Principle
  • Singly before pairing → Hund's Rule
  • Lowest energy first → Aufbau Principle
  • Positive ion smaller than atom → Cation formation
  • Negative ion larger than atom → Anion formation
  • Same number of electrons → Isoelectronic
  • Minimum frequency for electron ejection → Threshold Frequency / Photoelectric Effect
Watch Out

Common Mistakes

  • Confusing Z with A: Atomic number = protons (defines element). Mass number = protons + neutrons.
  • Wrong trend direction: Atomic radius decreases left to right. Ionization energy increases left to right.
  • Forgetting shielding: Inner electrons shield outer electrons from full nuclear charge → Zeff < Z
  • Ion size backward: Cations are smaller; anions are larger.
  • Orbital confusion: Aufbau ≠ Hund's ≠ Pauli. Know what each governs.
Practice Questions

Quick Drills

Question 1: Electron Configuration

Which of the following violates Hund's Rule?

A) ↑↓ | ↑ | — | —

B) ↑ | ↑ | ↑ | —

C) ↑↓ | ↑↓ | — | —

D) ↑ | — | — | —

Answer: C (electrons paired before filling all orbitals)

Question 2: Periodic Trends

Rank by atomic radius (largest to smallest): O, S, Se, F

Answer: Se > S > O > F (increases down group, decreases across period)

Question 3: Isoelectronic Series

Which is smallest: N³⁻, O²⁻, or F⁻?

Answer: F⁻ (all have 10 electrons; higher charge = smaller ion; F has highest nuclear charge)
