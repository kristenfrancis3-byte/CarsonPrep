Arts & Humanities – Core Visual Art Terms

High-frequency concepts and definitions you're likely to see in Big Questions and Quick Recall.

Frequency focus Definitions first Core vocab

Elements & Principles of Art

Foundational vocabulary
  • Line – a one-dimensional path of a point through space that usually defines a shape.
  • Color – an element with properties like hue (name of the color), value (lightness or darkness), and intensity (brightness or dullness).
  • Hue – the specific color on the spectrum (for example, red, green, violet).
  • Value – how light or dark a color is; artists use a value scale to make sure works have a full range.
  • Intensity – how bright or dull a color appears (also called chroma or saturation).
  • Texture – how an artwork feels or looks like it should feel (smooth, rough, etc.).
  • Balance – distribution of visual weight in a work (symmetrical, radial, etc.).
  • Contrast – strong differences (light vs. dark, big vs. small) that make things stand out.
  • Emphasis – creating a focal point where you want the viewer's eye to go.
  • Variety – using differences (colors, shapes, textures) to keep a work interesting.
  • Harmony – using similar elements so a work feels like it belongs together.
  • Movement – the path the viewer's eye takes through an artwork.

Painting & Drawing Techniques

Techniques that KAAC loves to repeat
  • Chiaroscuro light vs. dark – the interplay of light and shadow to make forms look three-dimensional.
  • Tenebrism extreme chiaroscuro – a very dark background with a strong spotlight on the subject (Caravaggio).
  • Impasto – paint applied so thickly that brush or knife marks are clearly visible.
  • Alla prima – "at the first"; completing a painting in one sitting, wet-on-wet.
  • Sfumato – very smooth shading with no harsh edges; "smoky" transitions (used by Leonardo).
  • Encaustic – painting with pigment mixed into hot wax, then fused with heat.
  • Fresco – applying pigments mixed with water onto wet plaster (e.g., Sistine Chapel).

Color Relationships

These often show up in Big Questions
  • Complementary colors – pairs opposite on the color wheel (red/green, blue/orange, yellow/violet).
  • Secondary colors – orange, green, purple (made by mixing two primaries).
  • Monochromatic – using only one hue plus its tints (with white) and shades (with black).
  • Tint – a hue plus white (makes it lighter).
  • Shade – a hue plus black (makes it darker).

Materials & Processes

Mediums and methods you should recognize instantly
  • Acrylic paint – water-soluble when wet, water-resistant when dry; fast-drying plastic polymer.
  • Gouache – opaque watercolor paint with a matte finish.
  • Batik – Indonesian resist-dye technique using wax on cloth.
  • Origami – art of paper folding (often one colored side, one blank side).
  • Glaze – glassy coating on ceramics that makes pottery waterproof and food-safe.
  • Bisque / biscuit ware – clay fired once but not yet glazed; still porous.
  • Lithography – printmaking technique using grease and water on stone or metal plate.
  • Screenprinting – (serigraphy) ink pushed through a mesh stencil with a squeegee.
