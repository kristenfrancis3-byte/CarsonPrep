9. Photoelectric Effect Equations

Overview

Threshold energy: E = h ν.

Kinetic energy of emitted electron: KE = h ν − φ, where φ = work function.

Pattern: If frequency < threshold → NO electrons, no matter the intensity.

Practice Questions

  1. 1. What scientist explained the photoelectric effect using photons?

    Albert Einstein

  2. 2. What must light exceed to eject electrons from a metal surface?

    Threshold frequency

  3. 3. What equation gives electron kinetic energy?

    KE = hν − φ

  4. 4. Increasing light intensity increases the number of electrons ejected but not what property?

    Their kinetic energy

  5. 5. If frequency is below threshold, what happens?

    No electrons are ejected

Early-Buzz Patterns

  • "This effect was explained by Einstein…" → Photoelectric effect
  • "This must be exceeded to eject electrons…" → Threshold frequency
  • "This equation subtracts work function…" → KE = hν – φ
  • "Light intensity changes electron count but not…" → Kinetic energy
  • "Below threshold, this process does NOT occur…" → Electron emission
