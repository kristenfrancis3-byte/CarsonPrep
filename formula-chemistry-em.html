3. Electromagnetic Energy Relationships

Overview

(KAAC LOVES wavelength–frequency–energy triangles.)

c = λ ν where c = 3.00 × 108 m/s, λ = wavelength, ν = frequency.

E = h ν and equivalently E = h c / λ, where h = 6.626 × 10−34 J·s.

Pattern: Shorter wavelength → higher frequency → higher energy.

Practice Questions

  1. 1. What equation relates wavelength and frequency for electromagnetic waves?

    c = λν

  2. 2. If wavelength decreases, what happens to photon energy?

    It increases

  3. 3. What constant has the value 6.626 × 10⁻³⁴ J·s?

    Planck’s constant

  4. 4. What equation gives the energy of a photon from frequency?

    E = hν

  5. 5. Which region of the electromagnetic spectrum has the highest energy?

    Gamma rays

Electromagnetic Energy – Buzz-in-First-Three-Words

  • “This equation relates…” → c = λν
  • “Energy of photons…” → E = hν
  • “Shorter wavelengths have…” → Higher energy
  • “This constant equals…” → Planck’s constant
  • “Gamma rays possess…” → Highest energy
