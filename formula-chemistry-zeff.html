6. Effective Nuclear Charge (Z_eff)

Overview

Definition: Zeff = Z − S, where Z = atomic number (protons) and S = shielding (core electrons).

Pattern: Across a period → Z increases, shielding ≈ same → Zeff increases → atoms shrink and hold electrons tighter. Down a group → shielding jumps → Zeff on valence electrons changes little.

Practice Questions

  1. 1. What formula defines effective nuclear charge?

    Z_eff = Z − S

  2. 2. Across a period, does Z_eff increase, decrease, or stay the same?

    Increase

  3. 3. Which causes greater shielding: core electrons or valence electrons?

    Core electrons

  4. 4. Higher Z_eff pulls electrons closer. What periodic trend does this directly explain?

    Decrease in atomic radius across a period

  5. 5. Why does Z_eff barely change down a group?

    Shielding increases as fast as proton count

  • "Calculated by subtracting shielding from nuclear charge…" → Z_eff

  • "This effect weakens valence electron attraction down a group…" → Shielding
