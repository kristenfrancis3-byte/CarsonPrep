Bonding & Molecular Structure

Overview

Classic Lewis steps: Count valence e− → connect skeleton → satisfy octets (H = 2) → add lone pairs → add double/triple bonds if needed → check formal charge.

Formal charge: FC = valence − (lone e− + bonded e−/2) → best structures have FC near zero with negative charges on electronegative atoms.

VSEPR electron domains → shapes: 2 = linear (180°); 3 = trigonal planar (120°); 4 = tetrahedral (109.5°); 5 = trigonal bipyramidal (90°/120°); 6 = octahedral (90°).

Polarity cue: Nonpolar if no lone pairs on central & all terminal atoms identical; lone pairs or different atoms → usually polar (except AX4E2 square planar can be nonpolar).

Hybridization shortcuts: Domains 2/3/4/5/6 correspond to sp/sp2/sp3/sp3d/sp3d2.

Practice Questions

  1. 1. What is the electron-domain geometry for a molecule with 4 domains?

    Tetrahedral

  2. 2. Which formula is used to assign formal charge?

    FC = valence − (lone e− + bonded e−/2)

  3. 3. CO2 has no lone pairs on the central atom and identical terminal atoms. Is it polar or nonpolar?

    Nonpolar (linear with equal pulls)

  4. 4. What is the hybridization of the central atom in NH3?

    sp3 (four domains)

  5. 5. When drawing Lewis structures, which atoms tend to appear terminal rather than central?

    Hydrogen and halogens (usually make one bond)

Buzz-in-First-Three-Words

  • “Counting valence electrons…” → Lewis structure setup
  • “Formal charge equals…” → FC formula
  • “Two domains produce…” → Linear, sp
  • “Central atom with lone pairs…” → Likely polar
  • “Trigonal planar geometry…” → 3 domains, sp2
