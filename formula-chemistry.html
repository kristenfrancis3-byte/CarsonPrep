Elite KAAC Buzzer Drills – Atomic & Periodic Patterns

These drills are designed so Carson can buzz within the first 1–3 words of a question. Read the first three words → answer instantly.

Subatomic Particles – Buzz-in-First-Three-Words

  • “This subatomic particle…” → Proton
  • “This particle differs…” → Neutron
  • “This negatively charged…” → Electron
  • “This number equals…” → Mass number
  • “Atoms become ions…” → Electrons change

Isotopes & Atomic Mass – Buzz-in-First-Three-Words

  • “Atoms of the…” → Isotopes
  • “Weighted average atomic…” → Average atomic mass
  • “This mass appears…” → Atomic mass
  • “Most abundant isotope…” → Controls average
  • “Same protons different…” → Isotopes

Electromagnetic Energy – Buzz-in-First-Three-Words

  • “This equation relates…” → c = λν
  • “Energy of photons…” → E = hν
  • “Shorter wavelengths have…” → Higher energy
  • “This constant equals…” → Planck’s constant
  • “Gamma rays possess…” → Highest energy

Electron Configuration – Buzz-in-First-Three-Words

  • “This rule states…” → Aufbau principle
  • “Electrons fill singly…” → Hund’s rule
  • “Opposite electron spins…” → Pauli exclusion principle
  • “This notation uses…” → Noble gas shorthand
  • “Unpaired electrons cause…” → Paramagnetism

Periodic Trends – Buzz-in-First-Three-Words

  • “This trend increases…” → Atomic radius
  • “Ability to attract…” → Electronegativity
  • “Energy required to…” → Ionization energy
  • “This trend decreases…” → Metallic character (across)
  • “More negative electron…” → Electron affinity

Effective Nuclear Charge – Buzz-in-First-Three-Words

  • “Calculated by subtracting…” → Zeff
  • “Increasing nuclear charge…” → Raises Z_eff
  • “Core electrons provide…” → Shielding
  • “Greater effective charge…” → Smaller radius
  • “Little change occurs…” → Down a group

Ion Size Patterns – Buzz-in-First-Three-Words

  • “Positive ions become…” → Smaller (cation)
  • “Negative ions become…” → Larger (anion)
  • “Same electrons different…” → Isoelectronic series
  • “More protons shrink…” → Radius decreases
  • “Gaining electrons increases…” → Repulsion (anion)

Quantum Numbers – Buzz-in-First-Three-Words

  • “This quantum number…” → n
  • “Subshell shape corresponds…” → l
  • “Orientation within space…” → ml
  • “Spin quantum number…” → ms
  • “Maximum electrons per…” → 2n²

Photoelectric Effect – Buzz-in-First-Three-Words

  • “Einstein explained this…” → Photoelectric effect
  • “Minimum frequency required…” → Threshold frequency
  • “Electron kinetic energy…” → KE = hν − φ
  • “Increasing intensity increases…” → Count of electrons
  • “Below threshold frequency…” → No emission

Nuclear Relationships – Buzz-in-First-Three-Words

  • “Difference between predicted…” → Mass defect
  • “This equation converts…” → E = (Δm)c²
  • “Most stable nuclei…” → Iron peak
  • “Energy holding nucleus…” → Binding energy
  • “Breaking nuclei requires…” → Binding energy input
